quantum chess rules

  • objective: checkmate the opponent's king
  • quantum mechanics: when you move a piece, your opponent doesn't see exactly where it went
  • superposition: the piece exists in multiple possible positions with probabilities
  • quantum collapse: position becomes certain when you:
    • capture an opponent's piece
    • put their king in check
  • probability display: gold percentages show likelihood of piece being on that square
  • multiple pieces: if multiple pieces can be on same square, they stack vertically
  • castling: move king 2 squares toward rook (standard chess rules)
  • visual cues:
    • white pieces: bright white
    • black pieces: dark with gray outline
    • selected piece: green highlight
    • valid moves: yellow dots
    • check: red square on king
